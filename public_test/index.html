About me

I am a graduate research assistant at the department of BMB, SUST, working under Dr. Tanvir Hossain , who did his PhD at AIBN, UQ, where I worked as a research intern with glycoRNA. Besides that, I work on a collaborative project on cancer genomics with BMU, Dhaka, under the supervision of Dr. S. M.Rashed Ul Islam.

Having come from a mathematical family, I have always approached biochemistry from a translational perspective, connecting biology with computational techniques to solve biological problems. Initially, I learned various sites of computational biology such as scRNA-seq, ML, and neuroscience, from Preonath Chondrow Dev , work as a research officer in CHRF, Dhaka. In one of my current research projects, I am investigating some latent features, which are the molecular driver genes, controlling the regulation of a molecular pathway, using the VAE + IG framework of neural networks. Through mental simulation, one can understand by observing the functionality of a conductor—a latent regulator, who moves the baton to control the tempo of the orchestra. I leverage various wet lab techniques, for example RNA-seq and qRT-PCR, to validate my findings.

I firmly believe that maintaining a peaceful mind is essential for both living life fully and conducting research effectively. Sri Krishna says, “The mind can be your greatest ally or your worst enemy.” Nowadays, I have a strong desire to uncover the mysteries of our brain.
